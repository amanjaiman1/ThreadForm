# ThreadForm — Product Document

> **Vision:** Make custom apparel design as effortless as posting a story, as powerful as a pro design tool, and as trustworthy as a national print brand — purpose-built for the people who actually order in groups: clubs, fests, teams, and communities.

ThreadForm is the **Canva × Nike By You × Printful × Figma** of custom apparel. A visitor lands, drags a logo onto a hoodie, sees it spin in 3D, shares a link with their club, collects 40 orders, and checks out — all without installing anything or talking to a salesperson.

---

## 1. Business Goals

### 1.1 North Star
**Number of apparel pieces shipped per month** (the metric that captures real value creation for users and revenue for ThreadForm simultaneously).

### 1.2 Strategic Goals (12–18 months)

| # | Goal | Why it matters | Target |
|---|------|----------------|--------|
| G1 | Frictionless creation | Design abandonment is the #1 killer of apparel platforms | 60% of designer-starters reach a saved design |
| G2 | Group ordering as default | Group orders have 10–40× the AOV of individual orders | 35% of paid orders are bulk |
| G3 | Ad-supported free tier | Primary revenue is ads; the funnel must be huge and free | 500K MAU by month 18 |
| G4 | Print partner network | We don't own presses; reliability comes from vetted partners | 3+ regional partners, <2% defect rate |
| G5 | Viral share loop | Every shared design is a free acquisition channel | Share-to-signup K-factor > 0.4 |

### 1.3 Positioning

- **Against Canva:** We are apparel-native — real garment 3D previews, print-ready output, and built-in ordering, not a generic canvas.
- **Against Nike By You:** We are open and multi-brand — any blank, any print partner, your own logo, group ordering.
- **Against Printful/Printify:** We are design-first and consumer-friendly — no Shopify store required, no merchant account, just design → share → order.
- **Against Figma:** We are zero-learning-curve and outcome-oriented (a shirt at your door), not an open-ended pro tool.

## 2. User Personas

### Persona A — "Ria, the Club Lead" *(primary)*
- **Who:** 3rd-year student, heads the cultural club's events team.
- **Job to be done:** "I need 45 matching hoodies for our fest, branded with our logo, before next month — and I don't want to chase everyone for money."
- **Pains:** Collecting sizes, collecting payment, designers ghosting, MOQ confusion, last-minute changes.
- **ThreadForm wins by:** Shareable design link + per-member size/payment collection + bulk pricing + clear timeline.
- **Success:** Orders 45 hoodies in one weekend without a spreadsheet.

### Persona B — "Arjun, the Solo Creator" *(volume / ad audience)*
- **Who:** Student who wants ONE cool oversized tee with his own art.
- **Job to be done:** "Make my idea real, cheap, fast, looks pro."
- **Pains:** High single-unit cost, ugly mockups, shipping uncertainty.
- **ThreadForm wins by:** Beautiful 3D preview, instant single-unit checkout, free design tools (monetized via ads).
- **Success:** Designs and orders a tee in under 10 minutes.

### Persona C — "Coach Meera, the Team Organizer"
- **Who:** Manages a college sports team / society.
- **Job to be done:** "Numbered, named jerseys for 22 players, consistent branding."
- **Pains:** Per-player personalization (name + number), roster management, deadlines.
- **ThreadForm wins by:** Roster-based variant generation, per-member personalization fields, bulk dashboard.
- **Success:** Generates 22 personalized variants from one base design.

### Persona D — "Dev, the Startup Founder"
- **Who:** Early-stage founder ordering team swag / event merch.
- **Job to be done:** "On-brand merch for the team and our launch event."
- **Pains:** Brand consistency, invoicing, reorders.
- **ThreadForm wins by:** Brand kit (logos, colors, fonts), saved designs, easy reorder, GST/business invoice.
- **Success:** Reorders the same design for every new hire.

### Persona E — "Sam, the Browsing Visitor" *(ad monetization core)*
- **Who:** Arrived from social/search, no account, just curious.
- **Job to be done:** "Play with the designer, see if it's cool."
- **ThreadForm wins by:** No-login design sandbox, autosave-to-account prompt, ad exposure during exploration.
- **Success:** Converts to a saved design (account) or returns later.

## 3. Revenue Strategy

Revenue is layered so the **free, ad-supported funnel feeds the higher-margin order business**, with premium features unlocking later.

### 3.1 Stream 1 — Advertising *(Primary)*
- **Model:** Display + native ads in non-disruptive surfaces: template gallery, dashboard, inspiration feed, and post-design "while we prepare your preview" moments.
- **Principle:** **Never** place ads inside the active design canvas or checkout. Ads live in discovery and idle surfaces.
- **Inventory:** House ads (promote bulk ordering, premium) early; programmatic + direct apparel-brand sponsorships as MAU scales.
- **Targeting:** Contextual + first-party interest signals (apparel type, college, event category). No sale of personal data.
- **KPI:** RPM (revenue per mille), ad viewability, fill rate.

### 3.2 Stream 2 — Apparel Orders
- **Model:** Margin on every printed garment = (customer price − print partner cost − payment fees).
- **Levers:** Bulk tiers, premium garments (heavyweight, oversized), add-ons (embroidery, sleeve print).
- **Bulk economics:** Group orders carry the business — higher AOV, lower per-unit CAC (one organizer brings many buyers).
- **KPI:** Gross margin %, AOV, bulk attach rate.

### 3.3 Stream 3 — Premium Features *(Later)*
- **ThreadForm Pro (subscription / per-seat):** Brand kits, unlimited storage of designs, advanced export, ad-free experience, priority production, team workspaces.
- **Pro for Orgs:** Multi-admin org accounts, invoicing, approval workflows, custom MOQ deals.
- **Marketplace (future):** Designers sell templates; ThreadForm takes a cut.
- **KPI:** Free→Pro conversion, Pro MRR, net revenue retention.

### 3.4 Revenue Mix Evolution
| Phase | Ads | Orders | Premium |
|-------|-----|--------|---------|
| MVP | 70% | 30% | 0% |
| V2 | 50% | 45% | 5% |
| V3 | 35% | 45% | 20% |

## 4. Success Metrics

### 4.1 Acquisition
- Monthly Active Users (MAU) — **North star feeder.**
- Share-driven signups (K-factor).
- CAC by channel.

### 4.2 Activation
- **Design Start Rate:** % of visitors who place ≥1 element on a garment.
- **Design Save Rate:** % of starters who save (creates account).
- **Time-to-First-Design (TTFD):** target < 5 min.

### 4.3 Engagement
- Designs created per active user.
- 3D preview interactions per session.
- Returning designer rate (7/30-day).

### 4.4 Monetization
- **Ad RPM** and viewability.
- **AOV** (individual vs bulk).
- **Bulk Order Share** of revenue.
- Free→Pro conversion (V3).

### 4.5 Fulfillment & Trust
- On-time production rate.
- Defect / reprint rate (target < 2%).
- NPS and order CSAT.

### 4.6 Retention / Loyalty
- 30/90-day order repeat rate.
- Reorder rate for organizers.

### 4.7 Guardrail Metrics (don't sacrifice these for growth)
- Checkout completion rate.
- Designer crash/error rate.
- Refund rate.
- Ad-induced bounce (ensure ads don't tank activation).

---

## 5. Product Principles

1. **Design first, account later.** Never block creativity behind a login.
2. **The garment is the hero.** Realistic 3D previews build the confidence to buy.
3. **Groups are the growth engine.** Optimize relentlessly for sharing and collective ordering.
4. **Ads fund the free magic** — but never insult the maker mid-flow.
5. **Print-ready by construction.** Every design carries the metadata production needs (DPI, placement, color profile).
6. **Trust is a feature.** Transparent pricing, timelines, and proofs reduce the fear of ordering custom goods online.
